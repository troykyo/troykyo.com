Bio

Digital Artisan,

Intrepid Designer,

Cunning Artificer

Troy Nachtigall is an expert in Wearable Technology Design and Internet of Things. Troy specializes in technology and materials that relate to design. From IT networking to tubular knitwear manufacturing, computer pattern making to fashion collection dynamics, Troy strives to realize a future where there is little difference between Fashion and Technology. Troy’s studies carried him from computer sciences in the Rocky Mountains to Fashion Design in New York City.Then from there the Fashion production centers of Europe . Troy has designed garments, communications and systems for brands including Hugo Boss, Calvin Klein, Jean Paul Gaultier Jeans, Dan Ward and Emilio Cavallini. Italy expanded Troy’s design technique due to the multitude of materials/techniques such as Knitwear, Denim, Leather, Tailored suitings, Seamless dresses and technical fibers. This passion for exploration led Troy back into University when he was asked to teach and research Digital Modeling for Fashion Design at the IUAV University of Venice. Troy has taught many courses such as Wearable Technology, 3D Modeling/Printing, Interaction Design, Eco Design and New Media Graphic Design at Universities including the IUAV, Sapienza, Institute of European Design Florence, and ISIA Florence. Troy is a member of the Wearables for Italy Team that explores Wearable Applications in Florence and is founding member of Fab Lab Tuscany. Troy currently holds an ArcInTexETN PhD research fellowship in Textile Thinking for adaptive and responsive wearables on the scale of the body at TU/Eindhoven Wearable Senses.
